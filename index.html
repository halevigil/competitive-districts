Candidates

Higher → Democratic candidates tend to be more moderate on average, and they vary more from district to district.
Higher → Republican candidates tend to be more moderate on average, and they vary more from district to district.
Higher → Democrats deliberately run more moderate candidates in swing districts.
Higher → Republicans deliberately run more moderate candidates in swing districts.

Voters

Higher → voters punish ideologically extreme candidates more heavily, relative to how partisan the district is.
Lower → election outcomes track district partisanship and candidate ideology more deterministically. Higher → more random per-district swings.
