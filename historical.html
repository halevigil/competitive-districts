Historical House Seats and Election Margins, 2008–2024

Each row is one US House election cycle. Left: seats binned by the district's partisan lean (presidential margin for presidential years; Cook-style PVI with 2-to-1 weighting on the two prior presidential cycles for midterm years), coloured by who actually won the House race (blue = Democratic, red = Republican). Right: distribution of per-district House election margins (R% − D%) for that cycle. Bin width is shared with the simulator (CONFIG.histograms.district.binSize). The "median lean" tag in each title is the chamber-pivot district's lean relative to the popular vote (national presidential margin for pres years; the PVI rows are already in this form).

Sources. House results: FiveThirtyEight election-results (per-district winner each cycle, in the contemporary district lines). Presidential margin: The Downballot / Daily Kos Elections per-CD calculations (2024 in 2024-cycle districts; 2008/2012/2016/2020 in 2020-cycle districts).

For 2008–2016 the join between House (contemporary districts) and presidential margin (2020-cycle districts) is approximate — districts redrawn after the 2010 census don't match exactly, so a small fraction of seats per cycle are dropped from the chart. Match rates: 2008 ~316 / 326, 2012 ~402 / 408, 2016 ~429 / 435, 2020 ~429 / 435, 2024 ~429 / 435.
