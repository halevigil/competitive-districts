Historical House Seats and Election Margins, 1992–2024

Pick a US House election cycle from the thumbnails below and it appears full-size at the top, next to the latest simulator run, so the two are easy to compare side-by-side. Left chart in each pair: seats binned by the district's partisan lean (presidential margin for presidential years; Cook-style PVI with 2-to-1 weighting on the two prior presidential cycles for midterm years), coloured by who actually won the House race (blue = Democratic, red = Republican). Right: distribution of per-district House election margins (R% − D%) for that cycle. Bin width is shared with the simulator (CONFIG.histograms.district.binSize). The "median lean" tag is the chamber-pivot district's lean relative to the popular vote (national presidential margin for pres years; the PVI rows are already in this form).

Sources. House results 1998–2024: FiveThirtyEight election-results. House results 1992–1996: MIT Election Data + Science Lab (1976–2018 constituency returns). Presidential margin 2008–2024: The Downballot / Daily Kos Elections per-CD calculations (2024 in 2024-cycle districts; 2008/2012/2016/2020 in 2020-cycle districts; 2000/2004/2008 in 2002-cycle districts). Presidential margin 1992/1996/2000 in 1992-cycle districts: Atlas of U.S. Presidential Election Results by Congressional District (Kiernan Park-Egan / WWU, 2022), election data from the Almanac of American Politics (Barone & Ujifusa) and Dave Leip's Atlas.

Coverage. Every cycle matches the full 435 D/R seats against a partisan-lean source. Louisiana's federal elections (jungle primary + occasional December runoff) and the two court-ordered Texas mid-decade redraws (TX 1996, TX 2006) are absorbed by falling back from general to runoff to jungle primary on a per-district basis. Fusion-ticket candidates (mostly New York Republicans cross-endorsed on Conservative / Independence lines, and Democrats on Working Families) are tallied per-candidate so all of one person's ballot lines count together. Three Independents who held a seat and consistently caucused with a party are coloured by their caucus: Bernie Sanders (VT-AL, 1992–2006) → D, Jo Ann Emerson (MO-08, 1996) → R, Virgil Goode (VA-05, 2000) → R.

Caveats. 1994's PVI bin is single-cycle (just 1992) — 1988 was held in 1980s district lines and can't be projected cleanly into 1992-cycle boundaries. 2002's PVI is single-cycle for the same reason (1996 lives in 1992-cycle lines, 2000 in 2002-cycle). For 2008–2016 the join between House (contemporary districts) and presidential margin (2020-cycle districts) is approximate — districts redrawn after the 2010 census don't match exactly, so a few seats per cycle inherit a slightly-different neighbour's lean rather than their own.
